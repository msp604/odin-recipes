Kimchi Jjigae

Description

Kimchi-jjigae, also known as kimchi stew, is one of the most beloved dishes in Korean cuisine. When make people think of Korean cuisine, the first thing that comes to mind is kimchi, one of the most iconic dishes in Korea. Made from brined and pickled baechu cabbage, it is the centerpoint of this fantastic and flavourful dish.

Steps

The steps to making kimchi-jjigae will vary considerably depending on the region of the recipe and the tastes of the chef. The recipe we're going to introduce to you today is largely foundational, incorporating many commonalities between other recipes, while leaving considerable room for improvement. For the best results, seek professionals and learn to make your own kimchi! Credits to Chef Paik Jongwon for the inspiration behind this recipe.

Kimchi Jjigae
★★★★★

An easy, delicious classic perfect for when you the need comfort of a homecooked meal.

Prep Time
15 minutes
Cook Time
20 minutes
Total Time
35 minutes
Author
Paik Jongwon
Servings
1

Stock

  • Used water from washing rice, 3 cups (540 ml)

Stew

  • Pork cut (shoulder or neck), 130g
  • Shin-kimchi ("aged kimchi"), 390g
  • Green onion, large (or a leek), 3x
  • Garlic, 20g
  • Coarse gochugaru (coarse chili pepper), 8g
  • Fine gochugaru (fine chili pepper), 3g
  • Soup soy sauce, 10g
  • Seyu-jeot (brined shrimp), 20g

Cooking

  1. After making rice, keep 3 cups worth of the water after the 2nd or 3rd rinse. This water helps keep flavour within the dish and is very easy to make. You can also choose to make a dashi instead, or even fry and boil the pork first to create a stock.
  2. Dice the Shin-kimchi. Shin-kimchi> is key when making kimchi-jjigae. It is often translated as "aged kimchi", and typically refers to kimchi that has soured somewhat. This strong flavour is really essential for the soup. As Chef Paik Jong Won says, you can actually simulate the taste at home even if you don't have it by mixing a bit of vinegar with your kimchi after chopping it.
  3. Start by cooking the pork first, then as it browns begin to add the water. Add the seyu-jeot at this stage to help flavour the stock.
  4. Simmer for about 20 minutes. Doll out any fat or scum that rises to the top of the soup.
  5. Add shin-kimchi, garlic, and soy sauce. Make sure to use soup soy sauce, not table soy sauce: the latter is a lot weaker and more diluted, and you will need more of it to flavour the soup.
  6. After a few minutes, add the gochugaru> to the soupo.
  7. Finally, when it's ready, add green onion and hot pepper if you wish.
  8. Serve and enjoy!
